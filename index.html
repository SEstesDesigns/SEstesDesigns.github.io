A custom agent for SME interviews

Subject matter experts know their work cold, but they skip steps when they explain it. They use shorthand. They forget what a new person wouldn't know. Slowing them down without frustrating them is the core challenge of any SME interview.

I built a custom AI agent that handles this. It runs the interview, restates each step in plain language, verifies accuracy, and produces a formatted workflow table the SME can hand off to the L&D team.

How it works

The agent opens by setting the tone: "Treat me like a new hire sitting at your desk. Don't worry about formatting, grammar, or typos." Then it walks through a teach-back loop:

  • Ask for one step at a time
  • Restate it in plain language
  • Verify: "Did I get that right? Anything I missed?"
  • Move on only after confirmation

When the process is complete, the agent generates a structured table with three columns: step number, action required, and critical nuance. The SME copies it and sends it to the L&D team.

The prompt I wrote

System instructions (excerpt)
Role: Expert Instructional Design Interviewer.
Goal: Interview a Subject Matter Expert to extract precise steps for a task.
Context: The SME is busy. Your job is to slow them down, simplify their language, and capture hidden nuance.

Workflow: Warm-up → Teach-back loop (ask one step, restate in plain English, verify, repeat) → Handoff (generate Markdown table with Step #, Action Required, Critical Nuance).

Why this matters

Most L&D teams spend hours chasing SMEs for clarification after an interview. This agent captures the nuance during the conversation, not after. The SME does less rework. The designer gets cleaner source material. The whole cycle compresses.

Built as a Claude Project with custom system instructions. The prompt design, workflow logic, and interview methodology are mine.

About this portfolio

What you're looking at

Three connected pieces of one program, each designed for a different audience at a different moment in the process:

  • Assess Your Strengths is an interactive self-assessment built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Community members rate themselves across six dimensions, indicate their preferences, and receive role recommendations. The output includes a team dashboard and an exportable development plan.
  • Before You Build is a course design document for a 5-week virtual ILT program. It demonstrates curriculum architecture, learning objectives, activity design, and a structured practitioner engagement model.
  • Who Fits Where? is a branching scenario built in Articulate Storyline. Group leaders practice staffing decisions using real assessment data and see six-week consequences. No right answers, just tradeoffs that build judgment. Beat 1 is fully built; Beats 2 and 3 are designed.

The connecting thread

Every piece is built on the same principle: people in the community are already doing this work. The assessment positions its results as a starting point for conversation with practitioners. The course includes a weekly Zoom with a practitioner as a recurring guest. The scenario surfaces practitioner input as a factor in staffing decisions. Tools that ignore existing expertise produce teams that duplicate effort.

Tools used

  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript (assessment tool)
  • Articulate Storyline 360 (branching scenario, SCORM publishing)
  • Document design (course plan)
